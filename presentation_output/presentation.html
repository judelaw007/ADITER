Production Sharing Contracts (PSCs)

ADIT Energy Resources Module 3.04 - Study Note

1. Core Framework and Legal Foundations

1.1 Definition and Fundamental Characteristics

A Production Sharing Contract (PSC), also known as a Production Sharing Agreement (PSA), is a contractual arrangement between a host government (or its designated national oil company) and an internat

The state retains legal ownership of petroleum resources in situ and throughout production

The contractor assumes exploration and development risk and finances all operations

Cost recovery is provided through allocation of a portion of production ("cost oil/gas")

2. Technical Rules and Fiscal Mechanics

2.1 Cost Oil/Gas - Recovery Mechanism

2.1.1 Cost Recovery Ceiling

The cost recovery ceiling (or cost stop) limits the percentage of available production that can be used for cost recovery in any period. This ceiling ensures the government receives a minimum share of

Typical Ceilings:

Indonesia: 100% (post-2017 "Gross Split" PSCs have no cost recovery)
