Licence and Concession Regimes

1. Core Framework and Legal Principles

1.1 Introduction to Extractive Industry Fiscal Regimes

Licence and concession regimes represent the two primary legal frameworks through which sovereign states grant rights to extract natural resources (petroleum, minerals, natural gas) to private entitie

The choice between a licence and concession regime has profound implications for:

Resource ownership during and after extraction

Tax and royalty structure applicable to operations

2. Technical Rules and Tax Provisions

2.1 Concession Regime - Tax Structure

2.1.1 Royalties

Royalties represent the state's compensation for resource depletion. They are:

Ad valorem: Percentage of gross revenue (typically 10-20%)

Specific: Fixed amount per unit ($/barrel, $/tonne)
