Slide: Treaty Shopping and Anti-Avoidance

Treaty shopping involves structuring through intermediaries in treaty jurisdictions to access favorable rates not directly available. Modern restrictions include: Principal Purpose Test (PPT): Denies treaty benefits if obtaining benefits was "one of the principal purposes" and granting them would be contrary to treaty object (OECD BEPS MLI). Limitation on Benefits (LOB): Particularly in US treaties, imposes objective tests (publicly traded, ownership, active business). Beneficial ownership: Treaties deny reduced withholding unless recipient is beneficial owner – conduit companies without substance are disregarded. Substance Requirements.
